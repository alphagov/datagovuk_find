Get management information from HM Courts and Tribunals Service to understand workload volumes and timeliness at a national level. The data is for criminal, civil and family courts, and tribunals. Data tables are published monthly for every year since 2019, and include:

- receipts – the number of cases that have been received and entered onto the respective administrative system by the court or tribunal in the month
- disposals – the number of cases that have been completed with a final result in the month
- outstanding – the number of cases that have been receipted but not yet disposed of at the end of the month
- timeliness – an end-to-end measure of how long those cases have taken to manage through the court or tribunal system for cases completed in the month 

There’s a separate page with statistical datasets covering a range of modernised service components in the criminal, civil, family and tribunals jurisdictions. This information primarily focuses on the digital uptake, number of applications and user satisfaction of services reformed under the HMCTS Reform Programme.
